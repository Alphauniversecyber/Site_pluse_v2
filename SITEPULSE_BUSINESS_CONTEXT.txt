SitePulse business context
Repo-derived summary written on 2026-04-29

Important note:
This file is based on the current codebase, schema, routes, copy, and logs. It is a strong implementation summary, not a founder-confirmed business memo. I did not verify live production behavior, and I did not run a full build.

1. Current state

What looks fully implemented in code right now:
- Public marketing site for SitePulse with positioning, pricing, roadmap, changelog, auth pages, legal pages, and a free scan funnel.
- Authenticated dashboard with overview, websites, reports, billing, branding, and settings.
- Website CRUD, manual scan runs, scan history, scheduling, notifications, and plan enforcement.
- Scan pipeline for performance, SEO, accessibility, best practices, SSL, security headers, broken links, CrUX, uptime, and competitor monitoring.
- PDF report generation, Supabase Storage upload, signed download links, and email sending through Resend.
- Paddle billing flow with checkout config, transaction confirmation, webhook queueing, webhook processing, customer portal, cancellation, and refund logic.
- Team access and white-label branding for higher-tier accounts.
- Client-share dashboard via magic token links at `/d/[token]`.
- Google Analytics and Google Search Console connector routes for client dashboard data.
- Internal admin area for billing, reports, users, cron jobs, emails, errors, and website monitoring.

What looks incomplete, fragile, or not fully verified:
- Some lifecycle and engagement emails are intentionally disabled in code to stay within daily send limits.
- The app depends heavily on external env vars and services. Missing Supabase, Paddle, Resend, Groq, Gemini, or Google OAuth credentials will break parts of the product.
- PDF is treated as an area that can fail sometimes: the app uses a browser/Puppeteer renderer first, then falls back to a jsPDF renderer if needed.
- Local build/start reliability is not a safe signal here. I was explicitly told not to rely on build for this environment.
- TypeScript currently passes with `npm run typecheck`.

5. Payments

Payments are implemented with Paddle, not Stripe.

Implemented billing behavior in code:
- Paddle overlay checkout
- Checkout config route
- Checkout completion confirmation route
- Webhook signature verification
- Webhook queue table plus cron-based processing
- Paddle customer portal access
- Subscription cancellation
- 14-day refund request logic for the latest payment
- Special yearly-trial handling where refund UI is hidden until the first paid annual charge

Business meaning:
- Billing is not "still pending" in the codebase. It is a real integration with lifecycle handling.
- The product is clearly built around paid subscription conversion after the trial/free funnel.

6. PDF reports

Current PDF state:
- Implemented and central to the product.
- Report generation loads website, scan, branding, history, SEO audit, SSL, security headers, CrUX, broken links, uptime, and competitor data.
- PDFs are uploaded into a private Supabase Storage bucket.
- Reports can be downloaded through signed URLs and emailed through Resend.

Renderer setup:
- First choice: browser-rendered HTML PDF
- Fallback: structured jsPDF renderer

Interpretation:
- This is more mature than a prototype.
- It is not safe to call it "bug-free" because the fallback path and admin error logging both imply this area has had reliability issues before.
- The changelog says template-based PDF reports were improved in April 2026 for more stable rendering.

9. Business model

The business model in code and copy looks like this:
- Free top-of-funnel website scan preview
- Free or quasi-free lightweight workspace entry point
- Paid subscription conversion for ongoing monitoring, reports, and delivery workflows
- Strong emphasis on agencies, not general consumers

The actual funnel feels like:
- Run a free scan
- Use it in a pitch or review conversation
- Create account or log in to unlock/save the result
- Move into trial
- Convert to Growth or Pro for multi-site monitoring, reports, and brandable client delivery

Plan/value structure:
- Free plan validates demand and starts the conversation
- Growth standardizes agency delivery
- Pro turns SitePulse into a premium white-labeled client system

Important enforcement details in code:
- Free plan: 1 website, weekly scans, 30-day history, no PDF/email reports
- Growth: 5 websites, daily/weekly/monthly scans, 90-day history, PDF + email reports
- Pro: 30 websites, same scan frequencies, 365-day history, white-label, 3 team members, CSV export

10. Other context an AI should always know about SitePulse

Core positioning:
- SitePulse is repeatedly framed as an agency growth, retention, and proof-of-value system.
- It does not want to feel like a commodity SEO tool.
- The copy strongly prefers business impact over raw technical jargon.

Tone and product philosophy:
- Premium
- Agency-first
- ROI-first
- Plain English where possible
- Client-ready outputs
- White-label delivery matters

What the product is trying to help agencies do:
- Close more clients
- Retain them longer
- Show measurable value without building manual audit decks every week
- Look proactive before clients ask what changed

Important strategic identity:
- Free scan preview is not a side feature. It is a major acquisition mechanic.
- Reports are not just exports. They are part of the retention and sales workflow.
- Shared dashboards, branded delivery, and business-impact framing are central to the product identity.

Useful caution for future AI work:
- Keep copy aligned to agencies, retainers, proof, risk reduction, and premium delivery.
- Do not drift the product into broad "all-in-one SEO platform" messaging.
- Do not assume Stripe. Billing is Paddle.
- Do not assume Google login. Auth is Supabase email/password.
- Do not assume PDF is optional. It is a core deliverable.
